The one idea to hold onto

It regulates the use,
not the technology.

The Act almost never asks "is this AI?" It asks "what is this AI used for, and how much could that hurt someone?" The riskier the use, the stricter the rules — from light-touch transparency all the way to an outright ban.

So the same underlying model can be unregulated in a video game and heavily controlled in a hiring tool. Find the use case, find its risk tier, and you know the obligations.

"A spam filter and a system that decides who gets bail can use the same AI — the law treats them completely differently, because the stakes are."
That's the whole framework: four tiers of risk, four levels of obligation.

A parallel track

General-purpose AI gets its own rules.

Foundation models (think large language models) don't fit neatly into a single use, so the Act regulates them separately — by capability, with the European Commission's new AI Office as the direct enforcer.

All GPAI models

Baseline transparency

Technical documentation, a summary of training data, and a policy to respect EU copyright. In force since 2 August 2025.

Systemic-risk GPAI

The most capable models

The largest models carry extra duties: model evaluations, adversarial testing, incident reporting, and cybersecurity — because their reach makes failures systemic.

Article 99 · what it costs

Fines scale with both the breach and the company.

Three tiers, each "the greater of" a fixed cap or a percentage of worldwide annual turnover — except for SMEs and startups, who pay the lower of the two as a proportionality safeguard.

€35M / 7%
For the prohibited practices in Article 5 — the bright-line bans.
€15M / 3%
For most other breaches — high-risk duties, GPAI obligations.
€7.5M / 1%
For supplying incorrect or misleading information to authorities.

The 2026 timeline

Phasing in — and reshuffled.

The Act took force in 2024 but applies in waves. In 2026 the Digital Omnibus pushed several high-risk deadlines back while keeping the bans and transparency rules on schedule.

Struck-through dates are the originals; the chips show where the Omnibus moved them.

2 Feb 2025

Prohibited practices banned

The Article 5 bans (social scoring, untargeted face-scraping, most public biometric ID) became enforceable.

2 Aug 2025

GPAI model rules

Obligations for general-purpose AI models took effect, enforced by the AI Office.

June 2026

You are here now

Bans and GPAI rules are live; the Digital Omnibus was approved by Parliament on 16 June 2026, with Council adoption expected 29 June.

2 Aug 2026

Transparency duties (Article 50)

Chatbots and AI-generated content must be disclosed. not delayed

2 Dec 2026

New ban + output watermarking

A new prohibition on non-consensual intimate imagery, plus machine-readable marking of generative-AI output.

2 Dec 2027 moved from 2 Aug 2026

High-risk duties (Annex III)

Stand-alone high-risk systems — hiring, credit, education, law enforcement — must fully comply.

2 Aug 2028 moved from 2 Aug 2027

High-risk in regulated products (Annex I)

AI embedded as a safety component in products already under EU safety law.
